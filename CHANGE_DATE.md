# DVAI Bridge — Change Date Register

This file lists every Released Version of DVAI Bridge, its Public Release
Date, and its Change Date — the date on which that version automatically
converts from the DVAI Bridge Community Licence (DVAI-BCL v1.0) to the Apache
License, Version 2.0 (Apache 2.0).

This file is part of Deep Voice AI Limited's public commitment to the
eventual full open-source availability of DVAI Bridge. The Change Date
mechanism is structurally built into the DVAI-BCL v1.0 (Section 2); once a
Change Date has been recorded here, it cannot be revoked or moved backwards.

## How the Change Date works

Each Released Version of DVAI Bridge has a defined Public Release Date. On
the third anniversary of that release date, the source code of that version
(including all commits to that version's source up to its Public Release
Date) is automatically relicensed under Apache 2.0.

After the Change Date has passed for a given Released Version:

- That version may be used for any purpose — including Commercial Use —
  without a separate Commercial Licence, subject only to the terms of Apache
  2.0.
- The Bridge Attribution Mark requirements of the Community Licence
  (DVAI-BCL Section 4) cease to apply to that version, although the standard
  Apache 2.0 NOTICE requirements continue to apply.
- The Change Date affects only the specific Released Version; later
  Released Versions remain under the DVAI-BCL until their own Change Dates.

The full Change Date mechanism is set out in Section 2 of the DVAI Bridge
Community Licence v1.0 (see `LICENSE`).

## What this means in practice

If You started using DVAI Bridge v4.0.0 on the day it was released (15 May
2026), then from 15 May 2029 onwards You may continue using that same
version under Apache 2.0 without any commercial-licensing relationship with
Deep Voice AI Limited. You will, of course, miss out on any subsequent
Released Versions of DVAI Bridge that are still within their three-year
Community Licence window — but the specific version You have is yours, on
permissive open-source terms, indefinitely.

This is by design. We believe that the long-term direction of DVAI Bridge
should be unambiguously open source, and the Change Date mechanism is how we
commit to that publicly. It also gives You, as a user, a permanent exit
path: whatever happens to Your commercial relationship with us, You will
eventually have Your version of DVAI Bridge on open-source terms.

## Verifying a version's status

To check the licensing status of a specific version of DVAI Bridge at any
time:

1. Find the version in the table above.
2. Compare the current date against the Change Date.
3. If the current date is before the Change Date, the version is under
   DVAI-BCL v1.0.
4. If the current date is on or after the Change Date, the version is under
   Apache 2.0.

For any questions about a specific version's licensing status, contact us at
info@deepvoiceai.co.
